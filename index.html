01 — Philosophy

Design is a conversation, not a coat of paint.

“Every interaction should be intentional. Every pixel should solve a problem — or quietly stay out of the way.”

My process is rooted in user-centered design — iterative testing, accessibility audits, and close collaboration with engineering and product. I treat research as a first-class artifact, not a checklist.

The best work I've shipped started with the smallest, most honest question: what is actually getting in the way? Everything else follows from there.

02 — Process

How I work, in four movements.

01 / Empathize

Listen first

Interviews, observation, and shadowing — long enough to hear what people don't quite know how to say.

02 / Define

Sharpen the problem

Synthesize messy notes into a single, defensible point of view the team can actually design against.

03 / Ideate

Diverge widely

Sketch broadly, then prune ruthlessly. The strongest idea usually earns its place against three weaker ones.

04 / Prototype

Make it real

High-fidelity flows you can tap through, hand to a user, and ship — not slides about what we might build.
